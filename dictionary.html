Commonly Used Words & Phrases

(Dictionary)

This page will list a bunch of assorted MTG terminology and defines/describes what they mean

Some examples include:

  1. Key words like "flying", "trample", "first strike", "reach", "menace", and "deathtouch"
  2. Game formats like "Standard", "Commander", "Pioneer", "Limited", and "Pauper"
  3. Commonly used words & phrases like "Tap", "Toughness", "Power", "The Stack", "Mana", and "Priority"
  4. The different zones like "Battlefield", "Hand", "Library", "Graveyard", and "Exile"
  5. Other important words & phrases like card types, turn phases, etc

Key Words

  • Death Touch: Creatures with Death Touch can kill other creatures with any amount of damage.
  • First Strike: Creatures with first strike can deal combat damage before creatures without first or double strike.
  • Flying: Creatures with the ability to fly can only be blocked by other creatures with flying, or a creature within reach.
  • Menace: Cretures with menace cannot be blocked unless there are multiple blockers.
  • Reach: Creatures with the ability to reach can block flying creatures.
  • Trample: Creatures with trample attacks and is blocked, it has the ability to unleash excess damage to the defending player if it dealt lethal damage to all blocking creatures.

Game Formats

  • Commander: Each deck is led by a legendary creature.
  • Limited: Players build decks from a limited card pool.
  • Standard: Players build decks from the most recent release from Wizards of The Coast.
  • Pauper: Players build decks from only common cards.
  • Pioneer: Players build from sets starting from Return to Ravinca forward.

Common Phrases

  • Mana: The primary resource used to cast spells and activate abilities.
  • Power: The characteristic of a creature that represents their offensive capability.
  • Priority: Determines in which order players can take actinos and cast spells.
  • Tap: To turn a card sideways indicating that it has been used to perform an action.
  • The Stack: A zone in which spells and abilities waits. If a player casts a spell or ability, it is placed onto the stack.
  • Toughness: A charactertistic of creatures that represents a creatures ability to withstand damange.

Zones

  • Battlefield: The zone in which permanents (cards or tokens) exists once played.
  • Exile: The zone in which cards are placed once removed from play.
  • Graveyard: The zone for used, destroyed, or discarded cards.
  • Hand: The zone for cards that have been drawn but not played.
  • Library: The zone for a players deck. At the start of the game all cards are shuffled and placed face down.
