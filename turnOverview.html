Turn Overview

This is some filler text and should be replaced with something that introduces the topic and gets the reader interested in learning more.

Beginning Phase

Untap

The first part of every turn. If it’s your turn, untap all permanents you control.

Upkeep

Effects that happen at the beginning of the turn happen here. (Don’t forget it!)

Draw

Draw a card for your turn.

Pre-Combat Main Phase

  • Play a Land - You can play up to 1 land on each of your turns if you have one.
  • Play/Cast creatures, sorceries, enchantments, artifacts, planeswalkers, battles, and instants

Combat Phase

Beginning of Combat

Combat begins. This step allows spells and effects while no longer in a main phase. (No sorcery speed effects)

Declare Attackers

If it’s your turn, select which creatures you want to attack with. Remember that this taps them which means you can’t block with them later.

Declare Blockers

If you’re the defending player, select your blocks now. (Remember you can group block)

First Strike Damage

First strike and double strike creatures do damage

Damage

Creatures without first strike do their damage now. (This also includes those with double strike)

End of Combat

Combat is over. Some effects are triggered.

Post-Combat Main Phase

  • Play a Land - You can play up to 1 land on each of your turns if you have one.
  • Play/Cast creatures, sorceries, enchantments, artifacts, planeswalkers, battles, and instants

End Phase

End Step

One last chance to play instant speed spells and effects before the turn ends. Some effects are also triggered here.

Cleanup Step

Many effects last until “end of turn” and they end now. You also remove all damage from creatures and discard down to your maximum hand size. (Usually 7)
